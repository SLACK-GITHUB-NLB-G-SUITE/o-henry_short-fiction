It Covers Errors

Poetic fame can be won this way:

A If you happen to have not a thing to say, And you happen to be close-pressed for time,

And you can’t for your life get a word to rhyme, And your knowledge of English is somewhat small,

And you have no poetic turn at all,

And can’t write a hand anybody can read.

You are in a first-rate way to succeed;

For who in the world can mix things worse Than a popular writer of dialect verse?
