Futility

To be so near⁠—and then to vanish

Like some unreal creature of the sense; To come so near that every fiber, tingling, Makes ready welcome; then to surge Back into the recesses of the strange, Mysterious unknown. Ye gods!

What agony to feel thee slowly steal Away from us when, with caught breath And streaming eyes, and parted lips,

We fain would with convulsive gasp

And tortured features bow our frame

In one loud spasm of homage to thy spell!

But with what grief we find we can not do it; The dream is o’er⁠—we can not sneeze.
